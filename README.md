# CS-360
Collection of final work from CS 360


Briefly summarize the requirements and goals of the app you developed. What user needs was this app designed to address?
  The app needed to be able to store event details and login info into two separate tables in a database. The app need to have a log in page. It also needed to show details in a gridlike view. Functions for adding, removing, and editing events needed to be included. Lastly, the app needs to have        notifcation functionality.

What screens and features were necessary to support user needs and produce a user-centered UI for the app? How did your UI designs keep users in mind? Why were your designs successful?
  For this app I had a a total of ten different pages that a user would navigate to depending on what they selected. These included, login page, adding events, removing events, etc. I choose to have all these functions because I felt that they were basic needs for an app with our requirements. Every    page is used and serves a purpose to benefit the user. I felt that my designs were successful because of their simplicity and clarity. In my eyes nothing is over done or harsh to look at.

How did you approach the process of coding your app? What techniques or strategies did you use? How could those techniques or strategies be applied in the future?
  For me when beginning to code I first focused on navigation and ensuring that all the needed pages were accessible from other pages. After this I began to add functions onto the pre-existing design and then improve pages with any changes that were deemed to be needed. In the future I feel like        focusing on the basic structure and than moving from their will help me with getting started on projects especially when im stuck and don't know where to start.

How did you test to ensure your code was functional? Why is this process important, and what did it reveal?
  I used android studios built in emulator as well as the build function. I prioritized making sure no errors were being reported and than ran the emulator. Through the emulator I was able to see the app work virtually through a phone to ensure that the apps functions all worked correctly. This         process helped me with debugging and ensuring that everything I created functioned with its desired purpose.

Consider the full app design and development process from initial planning to finalization. Where did you have to innovate to overcome a challenge?
  Most of my challenges came at the actual implementation stage. There were design choices I had that I initially thought would be nice to have. Hoever, when implementing I chose not to use them due to redundancies or just not needing them to function. This alleviated some of my development time        because I did not have to figure out how to get that function to work, nor adapt other pages to the funciton.

In what specific component of your mobile app were you particularly successful in demonstrating your knowledge, skills, and experience?
  I felt like navigation was my strong point as navigating the app I feel like is pretty smooth and consistent.
